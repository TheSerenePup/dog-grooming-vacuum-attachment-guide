🚫 Avoid these vacuum grooming mistakes

  • Using max suction on the first try. Always start on the lowest setting to let your dog acclimate.
  • Going straight for the belly or tail. Begin on the back and shoulders, where most dogs enjoy being touched.
  • Forgetting the treats. Pair the sound of the vacuum with high-value rewards to build a positive association.
  • Skipping the pre-brush on matted fur. If there are tangles, gently work them out with your fingers or a dematting tool first.

✅ Your 5‑minute “zero mess” grooming checklist

Tick these off for a calmer dog and a spotless floor.

  • Set to low suction & attach grooming tool. Confirm lowest setting.
  • Let your dog sniff the (off) attachment. Treats involved.
  • Start on the back, with the direction of fur. Slow, long strokes.
  • Avoid face, ears, paws, and tail. Stick to the main body.
  • Empty the bin outside. Marvel at the fur you didn't have to sweep.

✨ Most dogs acclimate within 2–3 sessions. The gentle massage sensation often wins them over.
